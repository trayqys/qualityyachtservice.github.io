Teak Deck Maintenance: A Seasonal Guide for Caribbean Yachts

November 3, 2023 8 min read Seasonal Guides

Teak decks are a hallmark of luxury yachts, offering both beauty and functionality. However, the intense Bahamian sun, saltwater, and tropical climate present unique challenges for teak maintenance. With proper care, your teak can maintain its rich golden hue and provide decades of service.

Understanding Bahamian Climate Challenges

The Bahamas offers ideal yachting conditions but presents specific challenges for teak maintenance:

  • Intense UV Exposure: Year-round sunshine can cause teak to gray and dry out
  • High Humidity: Promotes mold and mildew growth in shaded areas
  • Salt Corrosion: Salt crystals can accelerate wear and discoloration
  • Temperature Fluctuations: Expansion and contraction can lead to cracking

Pro Tip:

Teak contains natural oils that protect it from the elements. Our goal in maintenance is to preserve these oils while protecting against UV damage and biological growth.

Seasonal Maintenance Schedule

Dry Season (November - April)

During the drier, cooler months, focus on protective measures:

  1. Monthly Cleaning: Gentle wash with teak-specific cleaner to remove salt and dirt
  2. UV Protection: Apply teak sealant with high UV-blocking properties
  3. Moisture Check: Ensure proper drainage to prevent water pooling

Wet Season (May - October)

The rainy season requires more frequent attention:

  1. Bi-weekly Inspection: Check for mold/mildew in shaded areas
  2. Gentle Brightening: Use eco-friendly teak brightener to restore color
  3. Enhanced Sealing: Reapply sealant every 2-3 months for maximum protection

Common Teak Problems & Solutions

Graying: Caused by UV exposure. Solution: Teak brightener followed by quality sealant.

Black Stains: Usually mold or mildew. Solution: Eco-friendly mold remover and improved ventilation.

Cracking: From excessive drying. Solution: Regular oiling and avoiding harsh cleaners.

What to Avoid:

Never use pressure washers on teak (they strip natural oils), avoid household cleaners (too harsh), and don't let spills sit (especially oil and wine).

Professional vs. DIY Teak Care

While basic maintenance can be DIY, professional teak care offers advantages:

  • Expert Assessment: Professionals spot early warning signs you might miss
  • Proper Products: Access to marine-grade products not available to consumers
  • Time Efficiency: What takes a weekend DIY can be done professionally in hours
  • Guaranteed Results: Professional services typically include satisfaction guarantees
